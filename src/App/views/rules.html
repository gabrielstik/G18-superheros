JARVIS RULES

The goal of the game is to reduce the life of your opponent's hero to zero to win the game.

A player can create a deck of 30 cards, he has chosen beforehand in his collection. He has the opportunity to play against a random player or invite a friend.
In the first round, players receive 8 cards, drawn randomly from the deck. They can modify some of them to avoid having big cards impossible to play at the beginning of the game.
He can place 5 visible cards and 3 hidden cards on the board. Once the card is played, it must be placed in the graveyard.

The player starts the game with 5 energy points: he can use a point of energy to play a card, or to draw one.
When you have more energy points you have to go through. At each turn, the player gains one point of energy.

    The strategy is based on the different powers of the characters, visible on the maps:
  • Attack : inflicts damage to the opponent
  • Defence : resists his opponent's attacks
  • Speed : allows you to attack the opponent more quickly (the player with the highest value of the cards in the highest cards attacks first)
  • Intelligence : makes it easier to resist attacks (the player's intelligence must be superior to that of the opponent)

Each card fights the opponent's card, located in front of the game board. The life bar is used to determine the winner: when it reaches 0, the opposing player wins. The hero is connected to the life bar: he is hit by attacks when the space occupied by a map is empty, and can reach it directly.

There are several aspects that can increase your chances of victory :

  • The scarcity of cards :
    Each card has a rarity. The rarer a card is, the more power it has. It is possible to create one of these cards thanks to the vibranium (see Shop).
      Rarity of cards from the rarest to the least rare :
    • Common (Gray)
    • Rare (Blue)
    • Epic (Purple)
    • Legendary (Orange)
  • Affinities :
    Each character has affinities and antagonisms.
    If the card is combined with an ally, a bonus applies (bonus to decide).
    If combined with an enemy, a penalty applies (malus to decide).
  • Secret cards :
    These are the 3 hidden cards, they correspond to bonuses or penalties affecting the values of the powers, according to the character.

Grades and ranks :

At the end of each game, players earn adamantium, which is the experience. Depending on his rank, a player who fights a player with less adamantium will get a low amount of adamantium and vice versa.

A ranking is organized according to the rank of a player, who can evolve according to the amount of adamantium he has.
When a new player registers, he belongs to the rank Wood.

    He can reach a higher rank by paying with adamantium :
  • Bronze rank costs 25,000 adamantium
  • Silver rank costs 50,000 adamantium
  • Gold rank costs 80,000 adamantium
  • Platinum rank costs 100,000 adamantium
  • Diamond rank costs 180,000 adamantium
